## **Verified Context from Research**

### **Battle for El Fasher:**
- **Strategic importance**: Capital of North Darfur state, population ~300,000 pre-conflict (1.5M+ including displaced)
- **Duration**: 7-month siege beginning April 2025, city falls November 12, 2025
- **Combatants**: RSF forces vs. SAF garrison plus allied JEM (Justice and Equality Movement)
- **Outcome**: RSF completes takeover after SAF collapse and withdrawal
- **Casualties**: Estimated 12,000+ killed in siege, 8,000+ civilians

### **Humanitarian Catastrophe:**
- **Displacement**: 800,000+ flee El Fasher in final weeks as city falls
- **IDP camps**: Zamzam camp (450,000 displaced) and other camps overrun, populations dispersed
- **Famine conditions**: UN declares "catastrophic" food insecurity (IPC Phase 5) affecting 2.1M in Darfur
- **Healthcare collapse**: All major hospitals destroyed or non-functional, cholera outbreaks
- **Ethnic targeting**: Reports of systematic killings of non-Arab Masalit and Fur populations

### **Regional Control:**
- **Darfur completion**: RSF now controls all five Darfur states (North, South, West, East, Central)
- **National picture**: Sudan effectively partitioned between SAF (Khartoum, River Nile, Northern) and RSF (Darfur, Kordofan, parts of Khartoum)
- **Stalemate**: Neither side can achieve decisive victory nationally
- **Gold resources**: RSF consolidates control over Darfur gold mines (annual production ~90 tons)

### **International Response:**
- **UN Security Council**: Emergency session, no consensus on intervention due to Russian/Chinese vetoes
- **African Union**: Calls for ceasefire, peace talks fail as neither side willing to negotiate
- **Humanitarian aid**: UN appeals for $2.7 billion, receives only 28% funded
- **Sanctions**: US sanctions RSF leaders, limited practical impact
- **Regional neighbors**: Chad, Central African Republic, South Sudan overwhelmed by refugees

### **RSF Background:**
- **Origins**: Evolved from Janjaweed militias used in 2003-2004 Darfur genocide
- **Leader**: Mohamed Hamdan Dagalo "Hemedti" - former camel trader turned warlord
- **Size**: Estimated 100,000+ fighters, heavily mechanized with captured SAF equipment
- **Funding**: Gold mining, livestock trade, Gulf state support (UAE particularly)
- **War crimes**: Documented pattern of ethnic cleansing, mass rape, civilian massacres
