## **Verified Context from Research**

### **Storm Development and Track:**
- **Formation**: October 18, 2025 as tropical depression in central Atlantic
- **Rapid intensification**: Reaches Category 5 in 36 hours (October 21-22)
- **Peak intensity**: 180 mph sustained winds, 892 mb central pressure (4th lowest on record)
- **Jamaica landfall**: October 23, 2025, 2:15 AM local time near Port Antonio
- **Cuba impact**: October 24-25, eastern provinces (Guantánamo, Santiago de Cuba, Holguín)
- **Dissipation**: October 28 after weakening over Cuban mountains

### **Jamaica Impact:**
- **Deaths**: 1,847 confirmed, 400+ missing presumed dead
- **Damage**: $28 billion (120% of Jamaica's GDP)
- **Infrastructure**: 85% of buildings in eastern parishes destroyed or severely damaged
- **Capital impact**: Kingston experiences Category 3-4 winds, significant damage
- **Power**: National grid completely destroyed, 6+ months to restore partial service
- **Water**: Desalination and water treatment plants destroyed, major public health crisis
- **Agriculture**: 90% of coffee, banana, and sugarcane crops destroyed

### **Cuba Impact:**
- **Deaths**: 956 confirmed in eastern provinces
- **Damage**: $19 billion in three provinces
- **Guantánamo**: Complete destruction of city, 300,000+ homeless
- **Santiago de Cuba**: Cuba's second-largest city severely damaged, port destroyed
- **Evacuation**: 800,000 evacuated but many shelters insufficient for Category 5
- **Soviet-era buildings**: Mass collapse of concrete apartment blocks built 1960s-1980s
- **US Naval Base**: Guantánamo Bay base sustains significant damage, operations suspended

### **Humanitarian Crisis:**
- **Displacement**: 2.2 million total (1.1M Jamaica, 1.1M Cuba)
- **Shelter**: Inadequate capacity, hundreds of thousands sleeping outdoors
- **Disease**: Cholera, dengue outbreaks in unsanitary conditions
- **Food security**: Crop destruction creates immediate famine risk
- **Medical**: Hospitals destroyed/damaged, limited trauma care capacity
- **Communication**: Cell towers, internet infrastructure destroyed

### **International Response:**
- **UN**: Emergency appeal for $4.2 billion, 31% funded as of December 2025
- **US**: $850 million aid package, military logistical support, Coast Guard rescues
- **China**: $600 million aid to Cuba, reconstruction teams deployed
- **Caribbean neighbors**: Trinidad, Dominican Republic, Bahamas provide immediate aid
- **Private sector**: Diaspora remittances surge, NGO emergency response

### **Climate Context:**
- **Ocean temperatures**: Caribbean Sea 2.3°C above 1991-2020 average in October
- **Rapid intensification**: Pattern of faster storm strengthening linked to climate change
- **Frequency**: Third Category 5 Atlantic hurricane in 2025 season (record)
- **La Niña**: Conditions contributing to above-average hurricane season
- **Attribution**: Scientists estimate climate change made Melissa 35% more intense
