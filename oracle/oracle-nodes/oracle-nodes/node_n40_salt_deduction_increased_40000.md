# Node N40: SALT Deduction Increased to $40,000 - Tax Policy Shift

## **Node Details**
- **Node_ID**: N40
- **Description**: The "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" of 2025 increased the state and local tax (SALT) deduction cap from $10,000 to $40,000 for joint filers, with phase-outs beginning at $500,000 MAGI. The change primarily benefits upper-middle-class homeowners in high-tax states while doing little for entry-level home buyers.
- **Verification_Status**: **Verified** ✅
- **Date**: 2025 (enacted)
- **Scope**: US tax policy with housing and economic implications

---

## **Verified Context from Research**

### **Key Provisions:**
- **New Cap**: $40,000 for joint filers ($20,000 married filing separately)
- **Previous Cap**: $10,000 (since 2017 TCJA)
- **Phase-Out Threshold**: Begins at $500,000 MAGI
- **Phase-Out Rate**: 30% reduction for each dollar over threshold
- **Full Phase-Out**: Reverts to $10,000 at $600,000+ MAGI
- **Sunset**: Returns to $10,000 in 2030 unless extended

### **Beneficiaries:**
- **Primary**: Upper-middle-class in high-tax states (NY, NJ, CA, CT)
- **Profile**: Homeowners paying high property and state income taxes
- **Requirements**: Must itemize deductions (standard deduction doesn't benefit)
- **Typical Savings**: Up to $10,500 for those in 35% bracket below phase-out

### **Limitations:**
- **Phase-Out**: High earners lose benefit ("SALT torpedo")
- **Itemization Required**: Many taxpayers take standard deduction
- **Housing Focus**: Primarily benefits existing homeowners
- **Entry-Level Excluded**: First-time buyers typically don't itemize

### **Planning Implications:**
- Consider income timing around $500,000 threshold
- IRA contributions can reduce MAGI and preserve deduction
- Business owners may use pass-through entity workarounds
- Property tax prepayment strategies available
