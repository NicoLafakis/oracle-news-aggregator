- **Pandemic Hangover**: Forbearance programs delayed inevitable defaults

### **Who Is Affected:**
- **Subprime Borrowers**: 6.6% of subprime loans 60+ days delinquent
- **Prime Borrowers**: Increasingly affected, indicating broad-based distress
- **Demographics**: Crosses income levels, not limited to low-income households
- **Geographic Spread**: National phenomenon, concentrated in auto-dependent regions

## **Critical Strategic Implications**

### **The Consumer Stress Signal:**
Auto loans are uniquely sensitive economic indicators because:
- Cars are essential for employment in most of America
- People prioritize car payments after housing and food
- When auto payments fail, the consumer is already in crisis

### **The 2008 Echo:**
Unlike the 2008 crisis (subprime mortgages), this crisis is driven by:
- Legitimate price increases (not speculation)
- Supply chain disruptions that elevated vehicle costs
- Interest rate policy rather than lending fraud
- Consumer overextension rather than lender malfeasance

### **The Economic Trap:**
- Consumers need cars for jobs
- Cars cost more than ever
- Interest rates make financing expensive
- Wages haven't kept pace with costs
- **Result**: Structural affordability crisis with no easy solution
